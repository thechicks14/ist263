Background

Travis Hunter is one of the most unique prospects in college football history, having earned the Heisman Trophy for his exceptional two-way play at the University of Colorado. Originally from Suwanee, Georgia, Hunter was a five-star recruit who initially committed to Florida State before flipping to Jackson State to play for coach Deion Sanders. When Sanders moved to Colorado, Hunter followed and has flourished in Boulder.

Hunter's rare ability to excel at both cornerback and wide receiver at the highest level of college football has drawn comparisons to legendary two-way players like Charles Woodson. His impact on both sides of the ball has been transformative for the Colorado program.

Scout's Analysis

Travis Hunter is a generational talent who brings rare versatility and elite-level production on both sides of the ball. His ability to dominate as both a shutdown cornerback and dynamic wide receiver is unprecedented in modern college football. While NFL teams will likely need to determine his primary position, his skill set translates exceptionally well to the professional level regardless of which side of the ball he focuses on. Hunter's work ethic, football intelligence, and natural talent make him a blue-chip prospect worthy of a top-five selection.

As a cornerback, Hunter displays elite coverage skills with the fluidity and technique to match up against the NFL's best receivers. His ball-tracking ability and instincts allow him to consistently make plays on the football. As a receiver, his route precision, hands, and ability to separate make him an immediate threat in any offense. The athleticism he brings to either position is NFL-ready, and his competitive nature suggests he will continue to develop at the next level.
